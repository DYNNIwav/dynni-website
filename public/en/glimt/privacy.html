Glimt — Privacy

Last updated: 23 June 2026

Glimt is built to be private by default. This policy explains, in plain language, what little data we handle, why, and the rights you have over it. Glimt is operated from Norway and follows the EU/EEA General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

Data controller: Glimt, contact paal@dynni.no

The short version

  • Your glimt (photos, videos, captions) and your weekly vekeglimt are end-to-end encrypted. We cannot see their content, even on our own servers.
  • We do not show ads, we do not track you across apps or the web, and we never sell your data.
  • There is no algorithm and no public feed. What you share goes only to the friends you chose.

What we collect and why

  • Email address — to create and secure your account, and to sign you in.
  • Username and (optional) avatar — so friends can find and recognise you.
  • Date of birth — to confirm you meet the minimum age (see "Children").
  • Friends and bål membership — to deliver glimt to the right people.
  • Glimt and vekeglimt — the core service. Stored only as encrypted ciphertext we cannot read.
  • Delivery metadata (who sent to whom, timestamps, read status) — to deliver content and show your inbox.
  • Device push token — to notify you of new glimt.
  • Reports you file, and evidence attached to them — to keep the community safe and meet our legal duties.

We do not collect your contacts, your location, advertising identifiers, or browsing behaviour.

End-to-end encryption

Each glimt is encrypted on your device with your friend's public key before it is uploaded. Only the intended recipient's device can decrypt it. Your private key is stored in your device's secure hardware keystore (iOS Keychain / Android Keystore) and never leaves your device. This means we, and anyone who might access our servers, only ever see unreadable ciphertext.

A consequence of real end-to-end encryption: if you lose your device and your key, past glimt cannot be recovered. Your account itself stays safe and tied to your email.

How long we keep things

  • Glimt are deleted after they are viewed, and unopened glimt are automatically deleted after about 30 days.
  • Vekeglimt are removed after roughly three weeks.
  • Account data (email, username, friends) is kept until you delete your account.
  • Report evidence is kept only as long as needed to review and act on the report, then deleted.

Who we share data with

We use a small number of processors purely to run the service, under data-processing terms:

  • Supabase (database, storage, authentication) in an EU region, GDPR-compliant.
  • Expo / Apple / Google deliver push notifications.

We never share your data with advertisers or data brokers.

Your rights

Under the GDPR you can access, correct, export, or delete your data, and object to or restrict processing. You can delete your entire account at any time from Settings, which permanently removes your account, friends, bål and glimt. For any other request, email paal@dynni.no and we will respond within 30 days. You may also complain to your local data protection authority (in Norway, Datatilsynet).

Children

Glimt is not for children under 13 (or a higher age where local law requires, such as 16 in some countries). We ask for date of birth at sign-up and block under-age accounts. See our Child Safety Standards. If you believe a child under the minimum age has an account, contact paal@dynni.no.

Changes

If we change this policy, we will update the date above and, for significant changes, let you know in the app.
